City Infrastructure

The backbone of a smart city — engineered for resilience, efficiency, and human dignity. From monorail transit to mangrove conservation, every system works in concert.

Infrastructure

Seven integrated systems

01

Transportation

An elevated monorail spine and electric bus rapid transit are planned to connect Sanctus Gardens City's districts, paired with waterfront pedestrian boulevards and dedicated cycle networks. Ferry connections link the city to the Port Harcourt mainland via jetty facilities on the waterfront.

Monorail (planned) Electric BRT Ferry access Cycle network
02

Smart Technology

4 Rivers, Business Town One's utility and smart grid network, anchors the city's approach to power, water, and waste monitoring. Help Stone, the municipal service hub, coordinates civic and utility operations across the district. Underground fibre connectivity is planned to every plot.

4 Rivers utility network Help Stone service hub Fibre connectivity
03

Sustainability

Sanctus Gardens City is targeting Platinum certification under both LEED and EDGE — an unprecedented standard for a Nigerian development. The city is designed around solar, hydro, and biodegradable energy sources with the ambition of operating as a net-plus, net-zero city. See our Sustainability page for the full framework.

LEED & EDGE Platinum target Renewable energy design Net-zero ambition
04

Marine Infrastructure

Jetties, slipways, and dry dock facilities are planned along the waterfront, supporting marine access and vessel servicing for the marina district. All marine infrastructure is being designed for tidal operation in the Bight of Bonny.

Jetties (planned) Slipways Dry dock facilities
05

Commercial Ecosystem

Business Town One's commercial core — Brews 132, US 25, and Deutey-Eight 18 — is designed to Grade-A standards for office, hospitality, and mixed-use occupiers, anchored by Fat Things on Lees and supported by Shadow, the district's structured parking facility.

Grade-A mixed-use design Anchor retail secured Structured parking
06

Research & Innovation

PS Ninety9, Business Town One's Research and Technical Institute, is designed to support applied research, incubation, and prototyping space for climate-adapted technology suited to the Niger Delta.

PS Ninety9 institute Incubator space (planned)
07

Leisure & Recreation

Lapis Lazuli — an aquatic centre, aquarium, and water theme park — anchors the district's recreation offering, alongside Meadow public park and Pause Play indoor entertainment. A waterfront promenade connects the leisure district to the marina.

Lapis Lazuli Meadow public park Pause Play
Landmarks

Signature structures

Icon

Sanctus Tower

Planned 325m+ mixed-use landmark tower. Observation deck, sky restaurant and bar, premium office floors, and a sky lobby with panoramic Niger Delta views. The defining vertical element of the SGC skyline.

Conservatory

The Conservatory

An iconic glasshouse conservatory anchored within the restored mangrove sanctuary. Houses a botanical garden, event space for 500 guests, and an environmental education centre.

Transit

Monorail Transit System

An elevated light rail system planned to connect all six districts, integrated with electric bus feeder routes and ferry schedules.

Marine

Marine Facilities

Jetties providing passenger ferry access to Port Harcourt, alongside slipway and dry dock facilities for marine vessel maintenance. Marina berths available for private and commercial vessels.
