City Infrastructure

The backbone of a smart city — engineered for resilience, efficiency, and human dignity. From monorail transit to mangrove conservation, every system works in concert.

Infrastructure

Seven integrated systems

01

Transportation

An elevated monorail spine links all five districts with 6 stations. Complementary electric bus rapid transit on dedicated lanes, waterfront pedestrian boulevards, and cycle hire networks. Ferry connections from three jetties to Port Harcourt mainland.

Monorail · 6 stations Electric BRT Ferry terminals Cycle network
02

Smart Technology

City-wide IoT sensor mesh monitors air quality, traffic flow, waste fill levels, and energy consumption in real time. Underground fibre backbone to every plot, intelligent traffic management, smart waste bins, and a central city operating platform.

IoT mesh Fibre to every plot City OS platform Smart waste
03

Sustainability

District-wide water recycling and rainwater harvesting. Solar-ready rooftops and smart microgrid backbone. Mangrove restoration along 12 km of waterfront. Waste-to-energy facility and district cooling system. Electric vehicle charging infrastructure at every parking bay.

Water recycling Microgrid District cooling Waste-to-energy
04

Marine Infrastructure

Three passenger jetties, two slipways for boat maintenance, dry dock facilities for marine servicing, and a ferry terminal with scheduled services to Port Harcourt waterfront. Marina with 60+ berths for private and commercial vessels.

3 Jetties Dry dock 60+ berth marina Ferry terminal
05

Commercial Ecosystem

Grade-A office infrastructure: raised floors, 1.8 kW/m² power density, chilled beam HVAC, dual fibre feeds, backup generation, and 24/7 security. Retail-ready ground floors with dedicated service yards throughout the business district.

Grade-A office spec Retail-ready ground 24/7 security
06

Research & Innovation

High-bay laboratory spaces (6m clearance), 3-phase power, loading docks, and gigabit fibre redundancy. The Delta Lab incubator provides shared wet/dry lab facilities, co-working, and demonstration space for climate-tech ventures.

High-bay labs 3-phase power Incubator space
07

Leisure & Recreation

Central Park (8 ha), waterfront promenade with shaded walkways, sports fields, outdoor amphitheatre, kayak launch, and event spaces with power and water connections. Community centres, libraries, and pocket parks in every district.

Central Park · 8ha Waterfront promenade Amphitheatre
Landmarks

Signature structures

Icon

Sanctus Tower

Planned 325m+ mixed-use landmark tower. Observation deck, sky restaurant and bar, premium office floors, and a sky lobby with panoramic Niger Delta views. The defining vertical element of the SGC skyline.

Conservatory

The Conservatory

An iconic glasshouse conservatory anchored within the restored mangrove sanctuary. Houses a botanical garden, event space for 500 guests, and an environmental education centre.

Transit

Monorail Transit System

Elevated light rail with 6 stations spanning all five districts. Frequency of 4 minutes peak / 10 minutes off-peak. Integrated with BRT feeder routes and ferry schedule.

Marine

Marine Facilities

Three jetties providing passenger ferry access to Port Harcourt. Two slipways and dry dock facilities for marine vessel maintenance. Marina with 60+ berths for private and commercial use. All marine infrastructure designed for tidal operation and climate resilience.
