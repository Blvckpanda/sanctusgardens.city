Sustainability

Sanctus Gardens City is targeting Platinum certification under both LEED and EDGE — a first for a Nigerian development at this scale. Every system is designed around renewable energy, water efficiency, and long-term climate resilience.

Our approach

Resource efficiency, environmental stewardship, future resilience

District Cooling

Centralised chilled water plant serves all districts — designed to reduce energy consumption compared to conventional individual AC systems across the district. Heat rejection uses greywater for efficiency.

Water Recycling

Water recycling is planned at Lapis Lazuli specifically, alongside district-wide rainwater harvesting infrastructure designed into every rooftop. Rainwater harvesting from every rooftop. District-wide dual-pipe system for potable and recycled water.

Solar-Ready Grid

Solar power generation is planned across Business Town One's mixed-use buildings, supporting the city's ambition to operate as a net-plus, net-zero development. Smart microgrid backbone integrates distributed generation, battery storage, and demand-side management for grid stability.

Mangrove Restoration

12 km of waterfront mangrove restoration creates a natural carbon sink, storm surge barrier, and wildlife corridor. The Conservancy serves as a research and education hub.

Waste-to-Energy

Organic waste processing and centralized material recovery are designed into Business Town One's operational plan, supporting the city's broader waste-reduction ambitions. Organic waste processed through anaerobic digestion for biogas. Non-organic recyclable materials sorted at a centralized material recovery facility.

Smart City OS

4 Rivers, Business Town One's utility network, is designed to support real-time monitoring of energy, water, and waste systems across the district. Data-driven optimisation of city systems and a digital twin for planning and maintenance.
