# Sanctus Gardens City — Investment Discovery Platform

## Register

brand

## Users

High-net-worth investors, real estate developers, institutional fund managers, and diaspora Nigerians evaluating a 2,500-hectare waterfront smart-city development in Port Harcourt. They arrive seeking vision, credibility, and financial substance — not a sales pitch. Context: desktop-first (due diligence happens on big screens), late evening or weekend research, emotionally invested in Nigeria's future but skeptical of local real estate marketing.

## Product Purpose

Present Sanctus Gardens City as a world-class mega-development with the editorial ambition of NEOM and the refinement of Aman. Capture qualified investor leads through a deterministic conversion funnel — homepage → masterplan → district → opportunity → consultation. Each page builds conviction; the funnel gates sensitive financial data behind lead capture. Success = qualified consultations booked, not pageviews.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious · Editorial · Luxurious

Voice is confident but never loud — the scale of the project speaks for itself. Tone is architectural: precise, aspirational, grounded in the physical reality of land and infrastructure, not speculative hype.

## Anti-references

- **No beige luxury cliché.** No marble textures, no Champagne gold, no generic "luxury living" signifiers.
- **No generic Nigerian dev site aesthetic.** No crowded brochure layouts, no heavy gradients, no loud primary colors, no stock-photography families smiling at cameras.
- **No estate-agent template.** No "3 bedroom flat in Lekki" energy. SGC is a mega-city; the presentation must match the scale.
- **No dashboard or SaaS UI.** This is an editorial prospectus, not a property listing portal. No market grids, no charts, no "compare" widgets.
- **No small-device-first cramming.** Spaciousness is a design principle; the page should feel as open as the city it sells.

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial over marketing.** Present like a luxury prospectus or architectural monograph, not a sales brochure. Copy is architectural journalism; imagery is cinematic; layout is generous with whitespace.

2. **Ambition through restraint.** The project is vast — 2,500 hectares, 325m tower, five districts, $billions in infrastructure. The design doesn't need to shout. Restraint in color, motion, and density signals confidence.

3. **Identity without cliché.** Nigerian development, global presentation. Reference NEOM, Aman, and luxury editorial — not local competitor sites. The identity comes through in the green/gold palette, the mangrove-waterfront geography, and the story of Port Harcourt's transformation.

4. **Content as architecture.** Every page is a room. The masterplan is the lobby, each district is a gallery, the opportunity pages are the boardroom. Content hierarchy, pacing, and reveals mirror the physical experience of walking through a master-planned city.

5. **Conversion with dignity.** Lead capture is a natural next step for an intrigued investor, not a pop-up ambush. Financial data is gated behind a consultation because that's how serious investments work — the gate itself signals that what's behind it is valuable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Body text needs ≥4.5:1 contrast at all times (the cream-on-charcoal palette was designed for this). Reduced motion: all reveals work as crossfades or instant transitions. The site's primary audience is high-net-worth — assume good vision and desktop-class devices, but don't exclude mobile or assistive tech.
