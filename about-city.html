Our Vision

Sanctus Gardens City is a generational urban platform — a purpose-built smart city on the waterfront of Ogbogoro, Port Harcourt, designed to redefine urban life in the Niger Delta.

City Philosophy

A city, not an estate

Sanctus Gardens City is masterplanned as a complete urban ecosystem — not a housing development with amenities, but a genuine city with districts, infrastructure, economy, and identity.

At 115.91 hectares with a projected population of 70,000, Sanctus Gardens City is designed to function as a self-sustaining urban centre: a place where people live, work, learn, innovate, and belong. Every system — from mobility to energy to waste — is designed for resilience, efficiency, and climate adaptation.

The Masterplan

Phased development

Phase 1 · Foundation & Launch — Q3 2026 – Q3 2029

Business Town One (BT-1)

Infrastructure backbone — roads, underground utilities, fibre, monorail station shells, water treatment, and district cooling plant. Residential Estate, Marina, and Commercial District initial plots and buildings.

Phase 2 — Q3 2029 – Q3 2032

Eden Marina

Innovation District labs and incubator, Leisure District completion, Sanctus Tower foundation and core, Conservatory completion. Full monorail and BRT fleet operational.

Phase 3 — Q3 2029 – Q3 2032 · concurrent with Eden Marina

Business Town Two (BT-2)

Sanctus Tower full completion (325m+), Landmark District activation, remaining districts reach full build-out. City OS fully deployed.

Phase 4 — Q3 2032 – Q3 2035

Still Waters Residential Area

Full city population of 70,000. Ongoing smart city upgrades, district infill, sustainability performance monitoring, and adaptive reuse as urban needs evolve.

Business Town One dates reflect confirmed investor timelines. Later-phase dates are estimated from official development sequencing and subject to change.

Smart City Strategy

Technology as infrastructure

City Operating System

A central platform integrating traffic, waste, energy, water, and air quality data for real-time city management and proactive maintenance.

Connectivity Backbone

Underground fibre to every plot, public Wi-Fi in all common areas, and IoT sensor mesh network covering every district for air quality, fill levels, and flow monitoring.

Climate Resilience Grid

District cooling, smart microgrid with solar integration, water recycling network, and flood-responsive drainage designed for extreme weather events.

Sustainability Goals

Designed for future resilience

Sanctus Gardens City targets net-zero operational carbon by 2040, with 100% wastewater recycling, zero waste to landfill by 2035, and 40% green space coverage across the city.
