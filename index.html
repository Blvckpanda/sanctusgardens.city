Our Vision

Where the Niger Delta meets tomorrow

Sanctus Gardens City is a purpose-built, mixed-use smart city on the banks of Ogbogoro Creek, Port Harcourt. At 115.91 hectares masterplanned for 70,000 residents, it redefines what urban life means in Africa's energy capital.

Conceived by Heri Homes and Properties Limited, Sanctus Gardens City is a generational platform for sustainable urban growth — marrying world-class infrastructure with the unique beauty of the Niger Delta waterfront. This is not a housing estate. This is a city.

Engineered for Tomorrow

Infrastructure at city scale

The backbone of a smart city: from monorail transit to mangrove conservation, every system is built for resilience, efficiency, and human dignity.

Smart Technology

IoT sensor mesh, city-wide fiber backbone, intelligent traffic and waste management, district cooling, and smart grid-ready infrastructure.

Monorail Transit

An elevated light rail spine linking all six districts, with dedicated electric bus rapid transit lanes and waterfront pedestrian boulevards.

Marine Infrastructure

Jetties, slipways, dry dock facilities, and a ferry terminal connecting the city to Port Harcourt and the wider Niger Delta.

Mangrove Conservatory

A restored mangrove sanctuary and research center — the green lung of the city and a living laboratory for coastal conservation.

Sustainability Pillar

Built for resilience

Every system at Sanctus Gardens City is designed around resource efficiency, environmental stewardship, and long-term climate resilience.

  • District-wide water recycling and rainwater harvesting
  • Solar-ready rooftops and smart microgrid backbone
  • Mangrove restoration along 12 km of waterfront
  • Waste-to-energy facility and electric vehicle infrastructure
  • Cool streets design and tropical building orientation
